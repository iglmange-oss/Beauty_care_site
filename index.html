Hydration Heroes: Hyaluronic Acid vs. Ceramides

While often used interchangeably, **Hyaluronic Acid (HA)** and **Ceramides** serve different, yet complementary, functions in skincare. HA is a humectant, meaning it draws moisture from the atmosphere and deeper layers of the skin, plumping the surface. Ceramides, conversely, are lipids that naturally occur in the skin barrier, acting like a 'mortar' to prevent water loss (transepidermal water loss or TEWL).

For most skin types, using both is the optimal strategy: apply an HA serum to draw moisture into the skin, followed by a ceramide-rich moisturizer to seal that moisture in. This dual-action approach ensures maximum hydration, elasticity, and barrier function health.
